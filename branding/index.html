05
PRINCIPLES
Color ratio
White is the default canvas. It's what a viewer sees most of the time — the ground of most surfaces, the breathing room between elements, the reset between moments.
Navy and off-white are both accent grounds, used in different amounts. Navy carries the structural weight — roughly 30% of most surfaces. Off-white is rarer, around 10%, for warmth and editorial moments.
Gold stays rare — a rule, a label, a small chip. The eye will most likely be drawn to it, so it's important that it doesn't overpower and is used to draw sparing attention.
RULES
White dominates. Navy carries structural weight. Off-white accents with warmth. Gold punctuates.
Navy outweighs off-white. Off-white is the lighter accent, used sparingly for warmth — never as the default ground where white would serve.

PRINCIPLES
Photo treatment
When a photo is in a layout, the photo is the message. Type, color, and graphic elements are supporting cast — not competing personalities.
Generally that means no color overlays on photos except navy — and only when navy is needed to keep white text legible. No gold tints, no duotones, no filters. Gold stays in the supporting type: eyebrows, rules, small accents.
Type only sits on a photo when a full navy wash or gradient gives white text enough contrast to read.
If the image is strong, the composition around it should be quiet. If the composition is complex, the image should be quiet.
UNTREATED
Full photo, no overlay.
For portraits, product shots, detail moments.
BOTTOM GRADIENT
For type in the lower third.
Heroes, sermon series, event posters.
FULL NAVY WASH
For centered type over photo.
Quote cards, CTA blocks, invitations.
PHOTO + CARD
Photo sits inside a colored ground.
Cards, modules, listings.
